About Me

Ethos

I wanted to become a PT to help women feel more comfortable, it sounds cliche I know but growing up I struggled with my weight and did everything in my power to get my size down, so I could 'fit in'. When I achieved my goal size, I was happy, but the attempts to 'fit in' didn't magically dissolve. It was through the respect for movement, the investment in my health and the belief that my body was honestly a prized vessel which I should treat with love, that made me able to block out all of the pressures and fears surrounding body image. I train women so that the exercise becomes a stress relief and a vice even, with the happy by-product of aesthetic changes. I do not encourage calorie counting and do not offer 'fast blasts' this is a journey and you need to be in it for the long haul.

Mission

I want women to treat their health and fitness with the importance most men treat their weekly kick about with their friends. Unfortunately, society depicts an image of health being synonymous with wealth. It doesn't have to be, movement is second nature to us as humans, we need to connect back to that. Value ourselves for the wonderful beings we are. I want every woman everywhere to have the tools to change her body for the healthier.

Why Pre and Post Natal PT?

I train women throughout their pregnancy and after they have had their baby! I specialised in this because as women we are marginalised all the time, let alone when pregnant/ a new mum. I wanted to help women retain their identities and achieve their best health. you can learn a little about my journey here
