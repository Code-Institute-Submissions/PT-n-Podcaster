Is it safe to exercise?

YES! Exercising is probably one of the safest things to do whilst your pregnant, the healthier you are, the healthier the baby will be and the stronger you are, the more luck you are going to have with the delivery AND when the baby arrives! But don't take my word for it..

Will I get fat?

Not many women know this, but during pregnancy it is only necessary that you put on 1-2 stones, (provided you are already average weight after falling pregnant ) I feel that the narrative of 'just getting fat' is harmful to women and reiterates the dismissal of women during and after pregnancy.

Will I get injured?

Whilst pregnant, your exercise abilities change day to day and trimester to trimester. Roughly.. through the first trimester you are physically not very different to when you were not pregnant, but you normally FEEL quite lethargic and nauseous, so its harder to motivate yourself to exercise and THAT IS FINE! Second trimester.. You are normally a bit more used to the extra blood in your body, the hormone changes the increase on toilet breaks and the hormonal changes, so you are more engaged with the idea of exercising, HOWEVER your body's capacity has changed.. but only slightly! Your centre of gravity has now shifted (your blanace isnt great), you should avoid lifting things over your head (it can illicit high blood pressure) you should not be 'chancing' lifting weights. If you are a gym goer, you may push a little heavier on leg day, but now you're pregnant, that is not advisable, especially if you are alone. Now by the thrid trimester, you dont want to be doing any exercises that are throwing your balance off and you also want to avoid high intensity cardio and the guidelines from the second trimester still apply. So in short.. as lomng as you are being mindful and listening to your body whilst exercising, you should not be fearful of injury, ideally you shjould be training with a specialist though, maybe me?

Will the baby get injured?

If you're following the above guidelines, no.

After having the baby, how soon until I can go back to the gym?

This is TOTALLY based on you. Guidelines suggest that 2 weeks after a completely healthy, complication free delivery and 6 weeks for a c- section delivery. However, you shouldn't pressure yourself to force it, whatever feels best. I have had clients that have trained with me 1 week after having their baby and clients who have waited 6 months. ‘Why can’t I get my flat tummy back?’ When you have your baby, your core separates to make room for the bubba. It takes a while (post pregnancy) for the core plates to fuse back together. ESPECIALLY if you have experienced Diastasis Recti throughout the pregnancy. This means that if you had a flat stomach to begin with, it may take some time to get it back, but isometric core strengthening exercises will help!
